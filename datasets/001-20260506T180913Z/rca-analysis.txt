## **2. Root Cause**
### **Confirmed: Frontend Service Serving 404/500 Errors Due to Downstream Orders Service Failure**
- The **frontend** service is returning **HTTP 404 (3.2× spike) and 500 errors** (dedicated error metric 06) during the anomaly window.
- The **orders** service shows **zero HTTP traffic** (metrics 01, 07) despite **high CPU usage (3.7× spike)**, suggesting it is **unresponsive to HTTP requests** but still running background processes (e.g., GC, retries, or health checks).
- The **catalogue** service exhibits a **latency spike (4.4× avg)**, likely due to **retries or timeouts** when calling the failed **orders** service.
- The **shipping** service also shows **zero HTTP traffic**, but since it depends on **orders** (e.g., for order fulfillment), its inactivity is a **secondary effect** of the **orders** failure.

### **Hypothesis: Orders Service Crashed or Deadlocked**
- The **orders** service has **no HTTP traffic** but **high CPU usage**, which could indicate:
  - A **deadlock** or **thread exhaustion** (e.g., stuck in a loop or waiting on a resource).
  - A **crash loop** where the service restarts but fails to handle requests.
  - A **dependency failure** (e.g., database connection pool exhaustion).
- The **lack of HTTP 5xx metrics** for **orders** suggests it is **not even accepting connections**, reinforcing the deadlock/crash hypothesis.

## **4. Impact**
### **User-Facing Issues**
1. **Failed Checkout Flow**:
   - Users attempting to **place orders** will see **500 errors** (frontend) or **404s** (missing order endpoints).
   - The **orders service** is unresponsive, so **no new orders can be created**.
2. **Slow Product Browsing**:
   - The **catalogue service** has **high latency (4.4× spike)**, likely due to **retries/timeouts** when calling the failed **orders** service.
3. **Shipping Unavailable**:
   - The **shipping service** is inactive, so **order fulfillment is blocked**.

### **Business Impact**
- **Lost revenue** (users cannot complete purchases).
- **Degraded user experience** (slow page loads, errors).
- **Operational blind spot** (no HTTP traffic from orders, but CPU is high).

## **5. Suggested Fix**
### **Immediate Actions**
1. **Restart the Orders Service**:
   - The **high CPU + zero HTTP traffic** suggests a **deadlock or crash loop**.
   - A restart may resolve transient issues (e.g., thread exhaustion, memory leaks).
   - **Command**: `docker restart orders` (or equivalent in Kubernetes).
2. **Check Orders Service Logs**:
   - Since **no HTTP errors** are logged, inspect **application logs** for:
     - `OutOfMemoryError` (Java heap exhaustion).
     - `Deadlock` or `Thread starvation` (thread dumps).
     - Database connection pool exhaustion.
   - **Query Loki**:
     ```logql
     {container="orders"} |= "error" |~ "OutOfMemory|Deadlock|Thread|SQLException"
     ```
3. **Verify Database Connectivity**:
   - The **orders-db** container shows **stable CPU/memory**, but the **orders** service may be failing to connect.
   - Check **orders-db logs** for connection rejections.

### **Long-Term Fixes**
1. **Add Circuit Breakers**:
   - Implement **resilience patterns** (e.g., Hystrix, Resilience4j) in the **frontend** and **catalogue** services to **fail fast** when **orders** is down.
2. **Improve Monitoring**:
   - Add **custom metrics** for:
     - **Database connection pool usage** (orders service).
     - **Thread pool utilization** (to detect deadlocks).
   - Set up **alerts** for:
     - **Zero HTTP traffic + high CPU** (orders service).
     - **HTTP 500 errors** (frontend).
3. **Review Orders Service Dependencies**:
   - The **orders** service may be **blocking on an external call** (e.g., payment gateway, database).
   - **Enable distributed tracing** (Tempo) to identify slow dependencies.
4. **Load Test the Orders Service**:
   - The **CPU spike** suggests **inefficient code** (e.g., N+1 queries, unoptimized loops).
   - **Profile the service** under load to identify bottlenecks.

### **Rollback Plan (If Restart Fails)**
- **Deploy a known-good version** of the **orders** service.
- **Scale down dependent services** (frontend, catalogue) to reduce retries and timeouts.
